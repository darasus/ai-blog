## Blueberry Pancakes With Whipped Cream

First up is this delightful blueberry pancake recipe. Pancakes themselves aren’t exactly the healthiest of breakfast options, but these are packed with protein and will keep you feeling full for longer. If blueberries aren’t your thing, try swapping them out for another type of berry. This recipe makes two servings, so it’s perfect if you’re trying to feed a few people. To make these even more indulgent, top them with whipped cream.

What you need

2 cups all-purpose flour

2 tsp baking powder

1 tsp cinnamon

2 tsp sugar

½ tsp salt

1 cup plus 2 tbsp milk

2 tbsp vegetable oil

¼ cup blueberries

Whipped cream, to serve

Prep time: 15 minutes

Cook time: 20 minutes

Yield: 2 servings

Nutritional Information

Calories: 639, Serving Size: 1 pancake, 
Fat: 12 g, Saturated Fat: 1 g, Carbohydrates: 89 g, Sugars: 16 g, Protein: 19 g, 
Fiber: 3 g, Sodium: 507 mg

Directions

First, grease a large non-stick pan over medium heat. Put the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, sugar, and salt into a large bowl. Next, pour in the milk and vegetable oil and mix until just combined.

Add the blueberries and mix until just combined.

Once your pan is hot, add 1/3 cup of the batter. Cook for around 2 minutes until bubbles start to form on the top and the edges have started to set. Then, flip and cook for a further 1-2 minutes until slightly golden.

## Banana Oat Muffins

If you’re an oatmeal fan, these banana oat muffins are right up your alley. They may not look like much, but they’re actually packed with nutritional goodness. Plus, they take just 20 minutes to make, so you can enjoy a healthy and filling snack at any time of day. This recipe makes six servings, so you can store them in the freezer and enjoy them whenever you feel like you need a boost of energy. If you don’t want to freeze them, you can keep them in an airtight container for up to three days.

What you need

1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour

½ cup rolled oats

1 tsp baking powder

½ tsp baking soda

1/3 cup brown sugar

¼ tsp salt

2 ¼ tsp ground cinnamon

¼ cup vegetable oil

2 medium bananas

2 large eggs

¼ cup milk

¼ cup chopped walnuts, to serve

Directions

Preheat your oven to 375°F and grease a muffin tin. In a large bowl, mix together the flour, oats, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, salt, and cinnamon.

Add the oil, bananas, eggs, and milk and mix until just combined. Once your batter is smooth, stir in the chopped walnuts.

Pour the mixture into the muffin tin and bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.

## Berry Crumble Bowl

This berry crumble bowl is the perfect way to kick-start your day. It’s packed with fiber and protein, meaning it will keep you full for longer. If you want to make this recipe even healthier, try swapping the sugar for a natural sweetener like honey. You can serve this for breakfast or as a healthy dessert. You can also try adding a scoop of your favorite protein powder for an extra nutritious boost. This recipe is great for feeding a crowd because it makes four servings. If you’d like to make it for one, simply halve the ingredients.

What you need

1 cup old-fashioned oats

2 tbsp whole-wheat flour

1/3 cup brown sugar

¼ tsp cinnamon

¼ cup chopped walnuts

1 tbsp butter

2 cups mixed berries

2 cups milk

¼ tsp vanilla extract

Directions

Put the oats, flour, sugar, and cinnamon into a small bowl. Add the chopped walnuts and mix until combined.

Melt the butter and put it in a large bowl. Then, add the oat mixture, berries, milk, and vanilla extract and stir until combined.

Put the mixture into two bowls and microwave for 3-4 minutes.

## Creamy Coconut Shrimp Soup

Coconut has many health benefits, so it’s no surprise to see it in this tasty coconut shrimp soup recipe. This is a great choice if you’re trying to lose weight as it’s packed with protein and is low in calories. You can make this recipe as spicy as you like by adding as much chili as you like. Alternatively, you can reduce the amount of chili if you prefer a milder soup. This recipe makes six servings, so it’s great if you’re feeding a crowd or want to freeze some for later.

What you need

1 tbsp olive oil

1 small onion, diced

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 tsp red chili flakes

1 can coconut milk

1 cup water

3 oz cooked, peeled shrimp

1/3 cup chopped cilantro

Directions

Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook for 3 minutes, or until softened. Add the garlic and chili flakes and cook for 1 minute, or until fragrant.

Stir in the coconut milk, water, shrimp, and cilantro and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 5 minutes, or until the flavors have combined.

Transfer to a blender and blend until smooth. Alternatively, use an immersion blender to blend the soup in the pot.
